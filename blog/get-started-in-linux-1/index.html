This is the first part of my getting started in linux series. This series is specially made for Linux beginners. The information in this series can be applied to any Linux distribution based on Ubuntu and Debian, like, ZorinOS, Linux Mint, Linux Mint Debian Edition, PopOs, etc. Here, I am going to use ZorinOS for this series. Here, I am going to show you how to make a bootable usb stick of ZorinOS.
At first, you have get the ISO file.

Getting Linux

These are the steps to get ISO file:

  1. Open your prefferd web browser like Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge and go to https://zorin.com/ . Click on the ‘Download’ button.

  1. Scroll down a bit and Click on the ‘Download - free’ button.

  1. Click on the small ‘Skip to download’ button.

The download should start. Wait for the ISO file to finish downloading.

Making a bootable usb stick

After downloading ZorinOS iso file, You have to make a bootable usb stick to install it. For that, you are going to use a tool named Ventoy. At first, you have to download Ventoy.

Downloading Ventoy

These are steps to download Ventoy:

  1. Open your prefferd web browser like Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge and go to https://www.ventoy.net/en/index.html . Click on ‘Downloads’.

  1. Click on the first file under the ‘File’ section.

  1. Scroll down a bit and click on ‘ventoy-[something]-windows.zip’ under the ‘Assets’ of the ‘Latest’ section.

The download should start. Wait for the file to finish downloading.

Installing Ventoy

After downloading Ventoy, you need to install it. You need to use a usb stick for this process. The existing data on the usb stick is going to get wiped in this process.

These are the steps to install Ventoy:

  1. Insert the usb stick in your computer.

  2. Extract the downloaded file.

  1. Open ‘Ventoy2Disk’ exe application file.

  1. Select your usb stick under the ‘Device’ dropdown menu.

  1. Deselect ‘Secure Boot Support’ by clicking on it from the ‘Option’ menu under the menu bar.

  1. Select ‘GPT’ under ‘Partition Style’.

  1. Click on the ‘Install’ button.

  1. Click on ‘Yes’.

  1. Click on ‘Yes’ again.

  1. The installation should start. Wait for the installation to finish.

  2. Copy the ZorinOS iso file to the newly created partition.

You have successfully created a bootable usb stick of ZorinOS. The next part will be about installing ZorinOS. Stay tuned for the next part. üëç
